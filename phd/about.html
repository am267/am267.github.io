About Me

I take a broad approach to UX via the intersection of design thinking, information science, and user advocacy, where research is key to meaningful design.

As a scholar-practitioner with dual graduate degrees in user experience design and information science, my interests include user experience (UX), usability, accessibility, open access publishing, and preservation. My experience includes full-cycle UX research and design practices, including user research, information design, information architecture, interaction design, usability, dissemination, SEO, and documentation. In addition to my strengths in digital publishing and preservation, my area of specialization is inclusive design—the accessibility and usability of information products and services.

What is inclusive design? According to the Inclusive Design Research Centre, "inclusive design is design that considers the full range of human diversity with respect to ability, language, culture, gender, age and other forms of human difference." Coupling inclusive design with human-centered design (HCD) is my framework for solving problems and innovating ideas. HCD, or design thinking, is an iterative and creative approach to understanding human needs by getting to the root problem of products, services, or systems. Applying both sets of these design principles enables me to create positive people-centered experiences across industries and disciplines. I intentionally design for human diversity (age, disability, language) and apply UX methods to drive human-centered discovery, design, evaluation, and implementation of technology for the social good.
